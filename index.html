The Chronicles

ANCIENT LEGACY

AEGON'S CONQUEST
2 BC

Aegon I Targaryen, along with his sister-wives Visenya and Rhaenys, descended upon Westeros with their three dragons: Balerion, Vhagar, and Meraxes. From the burning of Harrenhal to the Field of Fire, they united six of the Seven Kingdoms under one crown, forging the Iron Throne from the swords of their fallen enemies, quenched in dragonflame.

THE GREAT COUNCIL
101 AC

Following the death of Prince Aemon, King Jaehaerys I summoned over a thousand lords to Harrenhal to prevent a civil war. In a choice that would haunt the dynasty for generations, the Council rejected the claim of Princess Rhaenys and her son Laenor, favoring Prince Viserys Targaryen. This established the precedent that the Iron Throne could not pass to a woman, nor through a woman to her male descendants.

BATTLE AT ROOK'S REST
129 AC

The first true clash of the Titans. Princess Rhaenys and her dragon Meleys were ambushed by King Aegon II on Sunfyre and Prince Aemond on the monstrous Vhagar. The sky burned as Meleys fell, but the cost was high—the King was left broken and charred, and the Green faction lost its momentum in a sea of dragonfire.

THE HEIR'S TOURNAMENT
112 AC

A celebration of blood and loss. While knights tilted in the streets of King's Landing, Queen Aemma Arryn died in childbed, leaving King Viserys without a male heir. In his grief and desperation, he named Princess Rhaenyra as his successor, shattering centuries of tradition and sowing the seeds of civil war.

DANCE OF THE DRAGONS
129 AC

Upon the death of King Viserys I, the realm split into two factions: the Blacks, led by Queen Rhaenyra, and the Greens, supporting King Aegon II. This brutal civil war saw brother fight brother and dragon tear dragon across the Seven Kingdoms. It was a dance of death that decimated the Targaryen bloodline and led to the tragic extinction of the world's last remaining dragons.
